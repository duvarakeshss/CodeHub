Welcome to PSG College of Technology

PSG College of Technology, an ISO 9001:2015 certified institution is one of the foremost institutions founded by the PSG & Sons' Charities Trust (1926). The College was established in the year 1951 and the Founders wisely decided to locate it in the same campus as the PSG Industrial Institute for effective industry-institute interaction.

Provide world-class Engineering Education, Foster Research and Development. Evolve innovative applications of Technology. Encourage Entrepreneurship. Ultimately mould young men and women capable of assuming leadership of the society for the betterment of the Country.

    The First Tech Challenge Awards 2017


    The First Tech Challenge was conducted by Caterpillar, India Limited Chennai at on 10.06.2017. Teams from PSG College of Technology, CEG, MIT, GCT, Thiagarajar College of Engineering, and St.Xavier's Catholic College of Engineering (Nagercoil) participated in the event. Each college has two teams (A and B) consisting of six students each. Teams from our college were guided by Dr.K.V.Anusuya, Assistant Professor, Department of ECE. The PSG team B comprising of Rohan Xavier Sequeira (Team Captain) (III-ECE), Adithya.J.J (III-Automobile), Jagadheesvaran.T.S (III-ICE), Harivignesh.A (II-ECE), Mohamed Khizam (II-Mechanical) and Siddharth Ganesh Chandrasekar (II-CSE) won the 'Overall Champion' and 'Winning Alliance' awards. The team was mentored by Ms.Saranya.A.M from Caterpillar India. The overall champion award was awarded based on total points scored during the tournament, interview performance, engineering notebook maintenance and a technical report submitted. The winning Alliance award was awarded to the two teams that won the final match of the tournament.

    DANFOSS INNOVATOR PROJECT AWARD 2016-17


    Two of our students from the ECE Department PraneshC (14L406)and VikasV (13L155)participated in the Danfoss Innovator Project Award 2016-17 and won the 1st prize in the UG category. Danfoss Innovator Project Award is an Inter-college competition designed for final year students in Under Graduate (UG) and Post Graduate (PG) courses. The participants were asked to choose problems from a theme of topics relevant to Danfoss' business. Theirproject, Mobile app based harmonic distortion analyzerprovides a user friendly and cost-effective solution for power quality monitoring. After the initial screening, the shortlisted nominations were reviewed by the Jury. A cash prize of Rs.1,50,000/- was awarded to them during the prize distribution ceremony held in Chennai.
    Faculty Guide: Dr. D. Sivaraj, Assistant Professor, ECE Department
    Danfoss Coordinator : Dr.M.Sundram, Assistant Professor, EEE Department
